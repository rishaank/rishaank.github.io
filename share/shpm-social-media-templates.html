Fill in the blanks. Stay short.

Three reusable caption formulas — one per post type. Replace the highlighted spots with your specifics. Keep captions under 120 words on Instagram, under 250 on Facebook.

06 — Voice & Tone

Write like the museum sounds in person.

Do

  • Lead with the person, the place, or the story.
  • Use plain dates and times — "Saturday, May 16 · 2–4pm."
  • Write short sentences. Four or five per caption is plenty.
  • Name real people when you have permission — docents, visitors, donors.
  • End with a clear next step: "Link in bio," "RSVP by Friday," "Stop by this weekend."

Don't

  • Open with "We are thrilled to announce…" or similar corporate openers.
  • Use academic hedges — "arguably," "some might say," "it could be suggested."
  • Stack more than two emoji. Ideally zero or one.
  • Copy-paste press-release language. If you wouldn't say it to a neighbor, rewrite it.
  • Use more than seven hashtags on a single Instagram post.
07 — Posting Cadence

A sustainable rhythm.

A small museum with volunteer-run social needs consistency more than volume. This cadence is achievable by one person in 2–3 hours per week.

Platform Frequency Best Days Typical Mix (per month)
Instagram Feed 2× per week Tue / Sat morning 4 history · 2 event · 2 community
Instagram Stories As-needed Day-of & day-before events 4–8 during event weeks, 0 otherwise
Facebook 1× per week Thursday afternoon 2 event · 1 history · 1 community
